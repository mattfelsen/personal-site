Interactive Kinetic Sculpture

This human-like form serves as a commentary on the necessity of social interaction for mental well being. This piece utilizes input from infrared sensors and microphones to determine the degree of interaction versus isolation of its viewers. It responds by changing its mood, expressed by making or breaking eye contact, adjusting its color, and altering the tone and modulation of its voice.

Created with PIC microcontroller, C code, servos, microphones, and fiber optics.
